THE LITURGICAL CALENDAR OF THE VEILED DAUGHTERS OF ZION

Rites, Observances, and Duties for Each Lunar Phase

~3,000 words

PROLOGUE — THE WORLD AS A MOON-CIRCLE

The Mother of Mothers taught:

**“The sun governs the years, but the moon governs the soul.”**

The moon marks:

  • the tides of blood,
  • the cycles of women,
  • the rhythms of birth and death,
  • the timing of planting and harvest,
  • the thresholds of silence and revelation.

Thus the Order binds its rituals not to the solar calendar of kings and commerce, but to the lunar calendar of women and mystery.

Each lunar month is a sacred journey of four phases , and each phase holds a liturgy essential to the spiritual health of the Daughters:

  • New Moon — The Veiled Phase
  • Waxing Moon — The Ascending Phase
  • Full Moon — The Revealing Phase
  • Waning Moon — The Returning Phase

This calendar governs all rites of the Order.

I. THE NEW MOON — THE VEILED PHASE

“In darkness we listen.”

Timing:

From the first invisible sliver of the lunar cycle until the first visible crescent.

Theme:

Silence, introspection, hidden beginnings.

Color:

Black or deep indigo.

Element:

Dust.

Gesture:

Lowered palm.

1. The Rite of Listening (Primary New Moon Rite)

This rite is foundational. It is practiced by initiates, Mothers, and children alike.

Procedure:

  • The Circle gathers with no lamps , only starlight.
  • Each woman stands alone facing the East.
  • Three breaths are taken:

* Breath of entering, * Breath of emptying, * Breath of waiting.

  • A Circle Mother speaks the invocation:
“Let the hidden speak beneath the noise.”
  • Each woman opens her palms toward the earth

and listens for whatever rises— emotion, memory, intuition, or Name.

Purpose:

To begin the month emptied of the world’s noise.

2. The Rite of Forgetting

Women write a fear, false name, or unwanted memory on a potsherd (broken piece of clay). They bury it secretly before the crescent appears.

No one may ask another about her buried shard.

Purpose:

Release of burdens that obstruct the path.

3. Dance of Dust (New Moon Dance)

Performed barefoot.

Steps are small, grounded, circular. The dust stirred by the feet symbolizes the raw beginning of creation.

Meaning:

You start without form. The month will shape you.

4. Social Rule of the Phase

**No major decisions. No oaths. No confrontations.**

This phase requires stillness.

Circle Mothers say:

“The wise do not move during the hidden phase.”

II. THE WAXING MOON — THE ASCENDING PHASE

“Let strength gather.”

Timing:

From the first crescent to the night before the full moon.

Theme:

Growth, intention, clarity, strengthening.

Color:

Blue or silver.

Element:

Water.

Gesture:

Right hand over heart.

1. The Rite of Naming Intention

Each woman states aloud a single intention for the month:

  • something to build
  • something to heal
  • something to learn
  • or something to free herself from

This intention is not a goal but a direction.

It must be spoken with bare feet placed shoulder-width apart, symbolizing stability.

A Circle Mother affirms:

“Let your path become your teacher.”

2. The Water-Cleansing Rite

A bowl of clean water is passed around the circle.

Each woman dips two fingers in the water and touches her forehead.

This symbolizes:

  • memory washing,
  • clarity of perception,
  • preparation for illumination.

Historically such water-blessing rituals appear across the Levant, especially in Canaanite and early Hebrew traditions of purification.

3. The Dance of Water (Waxing Dance)

Movements:

  • arms sweeping like waves,
  • feet tracing crescent shapes,
  • breathing in unison.

This dance creates communal synchrony, aligning all members toward shared growth.

4. The Rite of the Three Questions

Each initiate must answer privately:

  • What truth am I avoiding?
  • What strength am I refusing?
  • What gift am I withholding?

These questions are not shared. They are meant to stir inner alignment.

5. Social Rule of the Phase

**Begin tasks. Repair relationships. Speak truth gently.**

This is the time to move outward, but with caution.

III. THE FULL MOON — THE REVEALING PHASE

“What is hidden becomes seen.”

Timing:

The night of the full moon and the night before/after (three nights of power).

Theme:

Revelation, prophecy, joy, clarity, community, embodiment.

Color:

White or gold.

Element:

Light.

Gesture:

Raised palm.

1. The Rite of Illumination (Primary Full Moon Rite)

Procedure:

  • The Circle forms the Circle of Seven ,

whether seven women are present or not; smaller groups mimic the shape.

  • Lamps are lit and placed at cardinal directions.
  • A Circle Mother declares:
“By this light we see what we could not see.”
  • Each woman may speak a revelation—

something learned, realized, uncovered, or healed.

This is the only phase in which personal revelation is shared aloud.

2. The Dance of Light (Full Moon Dance)

This dance is the highlight of the lunar month.

Movements:

  • rising arms as if lifting a lamp,
  • feet making a full circle,
  • a moment of motionless radiance,
  • a spiral exit.

The dance signifies the unveiling of truth and the acceptance of one’s Hidden Name.

3. The Rite of the Open Mirror

A polished bronze or obsidian mirror is passed around.

Each woman must meet her own gaze until her breath synchronizes with her reflection.

This is a near-universal ritual in ancient Near Eastern mystery traditions, used to stabilize identity and induce mild trance.

Purpose: to confront truth without fear.

4. The Feast of Fullness

Food is shared— especially:

  • figs,
  • dates,
  • wine,
  • barley bread,
  • honey.

These represent abundance, sweetness, and fruition.

No one eats alone during this phase.

5. The Public Dance of Shiloh (Full Moon of Summer Only)

Once each year, during the full moon of midsummer, the maidens perform the Dance of Shiloh in open field.

Historically rooted in Judges 21, this dance is fast, joyous, communal, and symbolizes collective resilience.

6. Social Rule of the Phase

**Speak openly. Act bravely. Celebrate fully.**

Restraint during the full moon is considered a spiritual mistake.

IV. THE WANING MOON — THE RETURNING PHASE

“Release what must return to dust.”

Timing:

From the morning after the full moon until the last sliver is visible.

Theme:

Letting go, introspection, endings, rebalancing, preparation.

Color:

Brown or grey.

Element:

Ash.

Gesture:

Palm over the belly.

1. The Rite of Unmaking

A written intention from the waxing moon is reviewed.

If fulfilled, the initiate burns the parchment in a clay bowl and buries the ashes.

If unfinished, she tears it into seven strips and releases them into wind or flowing water.

Both are acceptable. The Order never shames incomplete intentions.

The Mothers teach: **“Nothing is wasted. Nothing is lost. Everything returns.”**

2. The Dance of Ash (Waning Dance)

Movements:

  • slow clockwise turning,
  • fingers touching forehead → heart → earth,
  • a low humming tone.

This dance prepares the spirit for the return to silence.

3. The Rite of Memory

A single memory from the month is chosen to keep.

The dancer presses her palm to the earth and whispers the memory.

Symbolically, memory is planted like a seed to grow meaning over time.

4. The Rite of the Closed Door

A symbolic “door” is marked on the ground with a line of ash.

Each woman steps over the line, then wipes it away with her foot.

This represents:

  • closing a cycle,
  • releasing attachments,
  • ending conversations,
  • completing griefs,
  • preparing for the new moon’s silence.

5. Social Rule of the Phase

**Do not begin new work. Do not confront. Do not reveal. Do not swear oaths.**

This is the time to withdraw and prepare for the coming Veiled Phase.

V. THE THREE GREAT LUNAR FESTIVALS

Though each month contains its own cycle, three certain moons hold greater significance.

1. The Moon of First Mothers (First Full Moon of Spring)

Honors:

  • the ancestral Mothers,
  • the origin of the Hidden Names,
  • the first Circle.

Rite: Seven oil lamps are carried in procession. The Dance of Oil is performed.

2. The Moon of Fate (First Full Moon After Midyear)

Honors:

  • destiny,
  • calling,
  • the weighing of paths.

Rite: Women stand in two lines, mirroring the Two-Sister Formation. Each woman receives a whispered blessing.

3. The Moon of Rest (Final Full Moon Before Winter)

Honors:

  • endings,
  • rest,
  • the peace after harvest.

Rite: Women lay down cloaks or shawls symbolizing burdens released before winter’s silence arrives.

VI. THE YEARLY CYCLE OF ROLES

1. The Maiden’s Twelve Crescents

Every new initiate (a Maiden) must perform:

  • 12 Rites of Listening
  • 12 Rites of Naming Intention
  • 12 Rites of Illumination
  • 12 Rites of Unmaking

After one full lunar year, she is eligible for receiving her Hidden Name.

2. The Sister’s Twelve Veils

A Sister performs the dance of each veil once per year:

  • Dust
  • Ash
  • Water
  • Oil
  • Wine
  • Milk
  • Light

This maintains balance in her life.

3. The Mother’s Year of Embodiment

A Circle Mother chooses one veil to embody for a year as her teaching focus.

Visitors or initiates follow her instruction to learn the mystery of that veil.

VII. THE HIDDEN MOONS

These rare lunar events hold unique rites.

1. The Blue Moon (Second Full Moon in a Month)

Rite: The Dance of Binding is performed—to mend community division.

2. The Blood Moon (Total Lunar Eclipse)

Rite: All Daughters remain silent from moonrise to moonset.

The eclipse symbolizes the world’s attempt to swallow the feminine mysteries.

Silence during this time is considered protective magic.

3. The Black Moon (Second New Moon in a Month)

Rite: Initiation of Circle Mothers, the most secret ritual in the Order.

The details are not written. They are transmitted in breath.

VIII. THE COVENANT OF THE MOON-CYCLE

At the end of each lunar month, the Circle Mother leads the closing chant:

**“What waxed shall wane, what waned shall rise. The moon remembers what we forget.”**

The Daughters reply:

**“We walk the circle. We keep the pattern. We live the hidden truth.”**

With this, the month ends and the next begins in silence.
