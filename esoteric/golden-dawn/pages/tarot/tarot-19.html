The Sun

XIX
Upright Meanings:

• Illumination
• Joy
• Success
• Life force
• Clarity

Reversed Meanings:

• Dimmed light
• Blocked joy
• Temporary setbacks
• Loss of clarity
• Shadow self

Element: Fire
Planet: Sol (The Sun)
Color: Gold & Yellow
Hebrew Letter: Resh
Sephira: Hod/Malkuth
Tarot Path: Between Hod & Malkuth

The Sun is the triumph of consciousness, the full illumination of truth and self-understanding. Unlike the Moon's reflected light, the Sun generates its own radiance—it is the ultimate symbol of enlightened being. In Hermetic tradition, the Sun represents the Divine Will manifest in creation, the gold hidden within base matter. When this card appears, you are entering a period of clarity, success, and genuine joy. This is not temporary happiness but the deep contentment of a being living in full alignment with truth. The Sun teaches that this illumination is your birthright.

Meditation Practice: The Midday Sun

Duration: 12 minutes (best practiced at noon or when the sun is high)

Best Time: Noon, days of achievement, times of clarity

  1. Find a spot where sunlight is warm on your skin
  2. Feel the direct heat and light—no filters, no reflections
  3. Visualize this solar light penetrating into your heart
  4. It illuminates every shadow, every doubt, every hidden corner
  5. Instead of fear, feel liberation—there is nothing to hide from truth
  6. Feel your own being radiating like the sun, authentic and unveiled
  7. Know this is your natural state: clear, warm, radiant life
  8. Give thanks to the sun for this remembrance

Integration: You are meant to shine. Let your light be seen. Illumination benefits all who encounter it. The Sun teaches that authentic self-expression is a gift to the world.
