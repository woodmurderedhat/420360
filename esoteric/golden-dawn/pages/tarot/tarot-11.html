Wheel of Fortune

X
Upright Meanings:

• Good fortune
• Cycles
• Destiny
• Karma
• Divine providence

Reversed Meanings:

• Bad luck
• Broken cycles
• Powerlessness
• Chaotic change
• Resistance to fate

Element: Spirit
Planet: Jupiter
Color: Deep Purple & Gold
Hebrew Letter: Kaph
Sephira: Chokmah/Binah axis
Tarot Path: Eternal Cycle

The Wheel of Fortune represents the understanding that all existence cycles through seasons of ascent and descent. Jupiter, its planetary correspondence, brings expansion and the sense of providential good fortune, yet even Jupiter must learn that what rises must fall, and what falls will rise again. In Hermetic philosophy, the wheel is karma—the law of cause and effect operating across time. The card teaches that we are not passive victims of fate, but participants in an eternal dance. By aligning ourselves with the turning of the wheel, understanding cycles, we move with grace rather than against resistance.

Meditation Practice: The Eternal Wheel

Duration: 12 minutes

Best Time: During times of change or transition

  1. Visualize a great wheel, turning slowly before you
  2. See it passing through four seasons as it rotates
  3. Notice: birth, growth, decay, and death occurring at each turn
  4. Realize that your life is also a turning wheel
  5. You are riding this wheel—sometimes at the top, sometimes at the bottom
  6. Neither position is permanent; both are necessary
  7. Say: "I accept the turning of the wheel. I trust the cycle."
  8. Feel peace in the inevitability of change

Integration: In good times, prepare for change. In difficult times, know improvement is coming. All cycles serve the wheel's eternal turning.
